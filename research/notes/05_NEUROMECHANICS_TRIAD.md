# Neuromechanics Triad

MorphoSNN frames body-near intelligence as a neuromechanical control problem. Rather than treating movement as a simple execution step after high-level planning, the project focuses on the distributed layer where rhythm, sensory correction, and body mechanics interact.

## Core Equation

CPG rhythm generation
+ local sensory feedback / reflex-like correction
+ body mechanics / morphological computation
= body-near neuromechanical control layer for physical AI

This equation is a design framing, not a biological model or a validated robotics result.

## CPG Rhythm Generation

Central pattern generator abstractions provide local rhythmic structure for repeated motor behaviors. In MorphoSNN, these rhythms are treated as modular controller motifs that can later be implemented using SNN-style mechanisms or other lightweight rhythmic generators.

## Local Sensory Feedback / Reflex-Like Correction

Local feedback loops adjust ongoing motion using body-near sensory signals. These loops are reflex-like in the engineering sense: they provide fast correction close to sensors and actuators without requiring every adjustment to be routed through centralized planning.

## Body Mechanics / Morphological Computation

Morphology contributes to control by shaping the physical dynamics of movement. Compliance, geometry, segment coupling, passive dynamics, and actuator placement can reduce or reshape control requirements by letting the body carry part of the control burden.

## Body-Near Intelligence

MorphoSNN treats intelligence as a body-near control layer, not only as centralized high-level planning. High-level AI may specify goals, constraints, or task context, but robust physical behavior also requires local rhythm generation, sensory correction, neuromodulation, and morphology-aware adaptation at the interface between controller and body.

## Boundaries

The triad does not claim one-to-one biological replication, guaranteed zero-shot adaptation, or validated physical-robot performance. It is a public seed framework for organizing future controller abstractions, examples, and benchmarks.
