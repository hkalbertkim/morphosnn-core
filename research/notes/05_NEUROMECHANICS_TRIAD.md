# Neuromechanics Triad

MorphoSNN frames body-near intelligence as a neuromechanical control problem. Rather than treating movement as a simple execution step after high-level planning, the project focuses on the distributed control layer where rhythmic neural dynamics, sensory correction, and body mechanics continuously shape behavior.

## The Triad

1. CPG rhythm generation
2. Local sensory feedback / reflex-like correction
3. Body mechanics / morphological computation

## CPG Rhythm Generation

Central pattern generator abstractions provide local rhythmic structure for repeated motor behaviors. In MorphoSNN, these rhythms are implemented as modular SNN-based controller motifs that can be coupled, tuned, and distributed across body segments or actuator groups.

## Local Sensory Feedback / Reflex-Like Correction

Local feedback loops adjust ongoing motion using body-near sensory signals. These loops are reflex-like in the engineering sense: they provide fast correction close to the body without requiring every adjustment to be routed through centralized planning.

## Body Mechanics / Morphological Computation

Morphology contributes to control by shaping the physical dynamics of movement. Compliance, geometry, segment coupling, passive dynamics, and actuator placement can simplify control requirements by letting the body perform part of the computation.

## Body-Near Intelligence

MorphoSNN treats intelligence as a body-near control layer, not only as centralized high-level planning. High-level AI may specify goals, constraints, or task context, but robust physical behavior also requires local rhythm generation, sensory correction, neuromodulation, and morphology-aware adaptation at the interface between controller and body.
